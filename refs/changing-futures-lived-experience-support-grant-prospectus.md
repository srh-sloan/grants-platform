## Overview

The Changing Futures Lived Experience Support Grant funds a **single national lived experience provider** to work with partner organisations and the central government team to strengthen cross-area learning and ensure lived experience plays a central role in programme design, delivery, and system change — both locally and nationally — for people experiencing multiple disadvantage.

It supports the next phase of the Changing Futures Programme (2026–2029).

Administered by the Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government (MHCLG).

---

## Eligibility Requirements

**Organisation type:**
- Voluntary, community, and faith sector (VCFS) organisations in England
- Must have **at least 3 years' experience** working within, or supporting, the multiple disadvantage landscape — specifically embedding lived experience in operational and strategic systems
- Evidenced experience of:
  - Applying **co-production principles**
  - Supporting people with lived experience to shape services and policy
  - Delivering and coordinating lived experience activity at a **national level**

**Application limit:**
- Organisations may submit **only one application**
- If an organisation submits multiple applications, only the first submitted will be assessed

---

## Funding Structure

- **Up to £750,000 of revenue funding** over 3 years
- Approximately **£250,000 per year on average** (final annual profile depends on agreed start date)
- Funding issued **annually** — cost schedules must accurately reflect needs for each year
- **Year 1 funding** will not be released until **July or August 2026**, subject to submission of a Grant Claim Form

## Assessment Process

Applications are assessed in **three parts**:

1. **Eligibility** — Pass / Fail
2. **Evaluation** — scored against the published scoring framework
3. **Declaration** — Pass / Fail

Applications that fail either Pass/Fail stage, or score 0 on any scored criterion, are not eligible for funding.

---

## Scoring Framework

Each scored criterion is marked on a 0–3 scale:

- **0** — no or unclear response
- **1** — partial / limited response
- **2** — good response with evidence
- **3** — strong response with clear evidence

**Auto-reject rule:** Any criterion scoring 0 makes the application ineligible for funding.

### Weighted sections

Answers are capped at a **300-word limit per question**. Known weighted sections include:

| Section | Weight | Max score | Questions |
|---|---|---|---|
| Section A (capability / approach) | 25% | 75 | 3 × 300 words |
| Section B (delivery / approach) | 15% | 45 | 3 × 300 words |
| Section C | 10% | 30 | — |

(Additional sections and their weightings are set out in the full scoring framework document; these three are the sections explicitly identified in the prospectus and scoring framework.)

The assessment considers the extent to which proposals demonstrate the **capability, approach, and value** required to deliver the learning and support role.

## What the Grant Funds

- Coordination of a **national lived experience network** across Changing Futures areas
- Cross-area learning activity (events, peer support, thematic work)
- Embedding lived experience in programme design, delivery, and governance
- Supporting local areas to adopt and strengthen co-production practices
- Working in partnership with the MHCLG central team and the separate Changing Futures Learning and Support Grant holder

## Why this prospectus is in `refs/`

This shape differs from EHCF and Pride in Place in useful ways:

- **Single-winner grant** (one national provider) — not a competitive fund with many awards
- **VCFS applicants**, but with a specialist national-scope eligibility bar (3+ years embedding lived experience at a *national* level)
- **Three-stage assessment**: Pass/Fail eligibility → scored evaluation → Pass/Fail declaration
- **Word-limited answer fields** (300 words per question) — a concrete form-runner constraint
- **Revenue-only** funding at a single large total (£750k / 3 years), with annual claim-form gates
- **No partnership requirement** (one application per org, first-submitted only is assessed)

Useful as a test case for: multi-stage assessment flow, word-count validation in the form runner, and scoring configurations that combine weighted scored sections with hard Pass/Fail gates.
