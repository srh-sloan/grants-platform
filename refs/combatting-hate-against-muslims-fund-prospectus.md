## Overview

The Combatting Hate Against Muslims Fund (CHAMB) will provide funding to
monitor incidents of anti-Muslim hate and to support victims. The grant
supports the work of an organisation or a consortium of organisations that
wishes to map a more accurate record of hate incidents affecting Muslims
across England.

The fund responds to the highest-on-record level of religious intolerance
and targeted hate incidents faced by Muslim communities. According to
police-recorded hate crime statistics for the year ending March 2024,
almost 2 in 5 (38%) of all religious hate crimes targeted Muslims — a 13%
increase on the previous year. The fund also recognises significant
under-reporting of such incidents.

---

## Purpose

Victims of hate, particularly those from minority backgrounds, may
sometimes be unwilling to report incidents to the police. This grant
funds a trusted, non-police reporting route so that:

- Hate incidents affecting Muslims in England can be captured, mapped and
  analysed more accurately;
- Reports received can be analysed to identify the trends and drivers
  behind such occurrences;
- Victims can be offered appropriate support.

---

## Objectives

The key objectives for the grant recipient are to:

- Receive, record and analyse instances of anti-Muslim hate;
- Increase awareness of what a hate crime is, willingness to report it
  and the options available to those who witness or experience
  anti-Muslim hate;
- Make it easier for those who have been subject to anti-Muslim hate to
  report their experience;
- Monitor, record and report on online anti-Muslim hate;
- Facilitate appropriate support for victims;
- Analyse reports received to identify trends and drivers.

The reporting system must allow for, and encourage, the recording of
hate crimes and hate incidents that occur online or in the real world.

## Eligibility Requirements

**Who can apply:**

- A single organisation, **or** a group of organisations applying together
  as a consortium (with one lead accountable body).
- Non-profit-distributing incorporated organisations, for example:
  - Charitable companies
  - Companies limited by guarantee (including Community Interest
    Companies — CICs)
  - Community Benefit Societies
- Companies limited by guarantee must have been established for
  charitable, benevolent or philanthropic purposes.

**Track record:**

- A demonstrable track record of supporting victims of crime or
  discrimination, particularly Muslim people, **or** the capability to do
  so.
- Ability to understand and work with Muslim communities, with reach
  into diverse Muslim communities across England.

**Governance and values:**

- Willing to record and monitor instances of anti-Muslim hate crime in a
  way that is consistent with the government's working definition of
  anti-Muslim hostility / Islamophobia.
- Aligned with government values on hate crime, extremism, community
  cohesion and the value of inter-faith work.
- Able to demonstrate robust and transparent governance arrangements.
- Able to demonstrate appropriate safeguarding policies when working
  with children or vulnerable adults.
- Must disclose information on all government funding received and
  applied for in the last 2 years.

**Auto-reject:** Any application that scores 0 against any assessment
criterion will not be eligible for funding.

## Application Process

1. **Register** for the Combatting Hate Against Muslims Fund via the
   MHCLG access-funding portal.
2. **Complete the application form** (available from 7 April 2025) and
   upload a detailed business plan.
3. **Answer questions** covering the 16 assessment criteria set out in
   the prospectus and business plan checklist.
4. **Submit** by 18 May 2025 at 23:59.
5. **Attend an interview** if MHCLG requires further clarification
   (w/c 26 May or 2 June 2025).
6. **Due diligence** is undertaken on the applicant and any consortium
   members — financial standing, and checks for adverse media or matters
   of reputational concern.
7. **Award** confirmed via the Grant Funding Agreement in July 2025.

Application portal:
https://apply.access-funding.communities.gov.uk/funding-round/CHAM/REG

## Assessment Criteria

Applicants answer questions against 16 criteria, supported by a detailed
business plan. Each criterion is scored 0–3 and weighted to reflect its
importance to the programme. Scores are multiplied by weightings and
summed; the maximum total score is **300**.

**Scoring scale:** 0 = unacceptable, 1 = satisfactory, 2 = good,
3 = excellent.

**Auto-reject rule:** Any criterion scored 0 eliminates the application
regardless of total score.

**Confirmed weighted sections** (from the published prospectus):

| Section | Assesses | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Applicant and organisational credibility | Suitability of the applicant, track record, governance, reach into Muslim communities | 40% |
| Cost of proposal and value for money | Budget breakdown, cost-effectiveness, proportionality | 10% |
| Risk management and reporting | Approach to risk management and reporting to government | 10% |
| Details of proposal, data, delivery and communications | Remaining criteria across proposal design, case management systems, data and analysis, communications, deliverability and victim support (exact sub-weights set out in the prospectus; weights sum to 100%) | 40% |

The preferred bidder is identified as the application with the highest
total weighted score, subject to passing all auto-reject thresholds and
due diligence.

## Proposal Requirements

The business plan and application must cover:

**Legal status and governance**

- Legal status of the organisation and registration numbers (e.g. charity
  number, Companies House number, FCA mutuals number).
- Details of consortium arrangements, the lead accountable body, and any
  complexity in the legal structure.
- Evidence of robust governance and safeguarding policies.

**Skills and experience**

- Summary of previous work undertaken and the outcomes delivered.
- Evidence of partnership and collaborative working with stakeholders.
- Brief descriptions of key personnel, staff, board and volunteers, with
  their relevant experience and qualifications.
- Ability to understand and work with diverse Muslim communities.

**Proposal — reporting anti-Muslim hate incidents and victim support**

- Proposed reporting channels (online, phone, in person), including the
  technical skills required to deliver them.
- Case management system: specification, procurement, implementation,
  integration, testing, commissioning and maintenance.
- Data security and system reliability.
- Evidence that the reporting process and subsequent support are easy to
  use and inclusive for potential complainants, and that the user
  experience encourages people to report.
- Identification of key success factors for the project — which should
  be measurable, deliverable, practical and realistic.
- Arrangements for facilitating follow-up victim support, including
  working with local and national partners, and faith and belief groups.

**Data and analysis**

- The kind of data to be gathered on reported incidents, how it will be
  verified, what analysis will be undertaken and when.
- How data and analysis will be presented, including demonstrating
  geographical reach across England.
- Data sharing protocols that ensure GDPR compliance.
- How insights underpinned by the data could be shared with MHCLG
  quickly in the event of a sudden change in the level of reports.

**Communications**

- Preparation and implementation of a communications plan, with
  particular reference to diverse Muslim communities.
- Plans to increase awareness of what hate crime is and to encourage
  victims to come forward.

**Deliverability**

- A timetable setting out key milestones and timescales for
  implementing activity, summarised in a Gantt chart showing
  milestones and project target dates.
- Details of how the applicant would be able to start operations
  quickly after award.

**Risk management and reporting to government**

- A risk register listing potential risks associated with the proposal
  and the mitigating actions.
- Reporting approach to MHCLG, including frequency, content and escalation.

**Cost and value for money**

- Detailed, itemised budget covering all three financial years.
- Justification of costs and demonstration of value for money.
- Long-term financial sustainability beyond the advertised funding period.

## Grant Agreement and Post-Award Obligations

- Funding is made via a **Grant Funding Agreement (GFA)** between MHCLG
  and the lead accountable organisation.
- The GFA for 2025 to 2026 is confirmed at award; it will be amended for
  the second and third years if funding is confirmed by the Spending
  Review.
- The grant recipient must report to MHCLG in line with the agreed
  reporting approach, including performance data and financial reporting.
- The recipient must record and monitor incidents consistently with the
  government's working definition of anti-Muslim hostility /
  Islamophobia.
- All project costs must be paid by **31 March 2028**.
