## Overview

The Common Ground Award is a **£1.7 million capital grant scheme** administered by the Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government (MHCLG). It recognises and supports voluntary, community, and social enterprise (VCSE) organisations in England that build **bridging social capital** — social connection across and between different communities.

The award combines recognition of good practice with a small capital grant to scale that practice further.

---

## Eligibility Requirements

**Organisation type:**
- VCSE organisations with **a base and operating in England**
- Must have **charitable, benevolent or philanthropic purposes**
- Individual eligible organisations only — **no partnership or consortium bids**

**Ineligible applicants:**
- Local authorities and other public sector organisations
- Profit-distributing organisations
- Individuals
- Regulated education sector organisations (schools, universities, etc.)

---

## Funding Structure

- **Total scheme funding:** up to £1.7 million within the 2025/2026 financial year
- **Standard award:** up to **£10,000** per successful applicant, as a **capital grant**
- **No minimum** amount — applicants can apply for less
- **Exceptional scale-up:** applicants may outline how capital investment could be scaled up beyond £10,000, up to a maximum of **£50,000**
  - Only considered in exceptional cases
  - Subject to availability of scheme funding
  - Requires a high overall assessment score against the criteria

Funding must be **committed by 31 March 2026**.

## Assessment Criteria

Each criterion is scored **0–3**:

- **0** — no evidence
- **1** — limited evidence
- **2** — good evidence
- **3** — exceptional evidence

**Auto-reject rule:** Any criterion scoring 0 is ineligible for funding.

### Weighted criteria

| # | Criterion | Weight | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Strategic alignment | 25% | How the organisation's aims and activities align with the fund's core objective of building bridging social capital |
| 2 | Community engagement | 25% | How the organisation engages with communities and other organisations throughout its work |
| 3 | Community impact | 25% | Demonstrable outcomes — greater interaction, trust, or understanding between groups; new networks, partnerships, friendships; improvements in mental health, confidence, reduced isolation |
| 4 | Sustainability | 12.5% | How the organisation will continue to make impact into the future (longer-term plans, evaluation); used to help determine the **size** of capital grant awards |
| 5 | Use of funding | 12.5% | Reasonable use of the capital investment requested |

(Totals to 100%. Weightings per published scoring framework.)

## What the Grant Funds

- **Capital investment** that supports or enhances an organisation's ability to build bridging social capital
- Examples may include: physical improvements to community spaces, equipment, fixtures that enable cross-community activity

### Bridging social capital — definition

"Work that builds social connection **across and between** different communities" — as distinct from bonding social capital (strengthening ties within an existing group). Evidence of impact could include:

- Greater levels of interaction, trust, or understanding between different groups
- Formation of new networks, partnerships, and friendships
- Improvements in mental health, confidence, and reduced isolation for participants

---

## Partnership Rules

- **No partnership or consortium applications.** Only bids from individual eligible organisations are accepted.
- An organisation can of course *work with* other community groups — the restriction is on the applicant entity.

## Post-Award Obligations

- Use the capital grant for the purpose stated in the application
- Spend / commit funding by the scheme deadline (**31 March 2026**)
- Comply with the Grant Funding Agreement, UK subsidy control, and equalities legislation
- Participate in monitoring and reporting as required by MHCLG

## Why this prospectus is in `refs/`

This shape adds yet another useful variation vs EHCF, Pride in Place, Local Digital, and Changing Futures:

- **Small-award, high-volume** scheme (many £10k awards vs a few large grants)
- **Capital-only** funding (no revenue component at all)
- **Hard no-partnership** rule (opposite of Local Digital Fund's mandatory partnership)
- **Five weighted criteria** including a split of 25/25/25/12.5/12.5 — exercises the scoring engine with non-uniform weights
- **Sustainability score directly affects award size**, not just pass/fail — a data-driven rule where score drives funding amount
- **Scale-up clause**: applicants can flag £10k–£50k scale-up subject to high score + fund availability — a conditional award amount

Useful as a test case for: small-grant mass-assessment flows, capital-only budgets, award-size rules driven by specific criterion scores, and conditional / tiered award amounts.
